VROOM

Urban air pollution is one of the most pressing environmental challenges faced by modern cities. Among the various sources of pollution, road traffic is widely assumed to be a primary contributor. In cities like Milan and Bologna—both characterized by dense traffic and periodic air quality alerts—it seems intuitive to link vehicle circulation directly to the deterioration of air quality. However, while this correlation is often taken for granted in public discourse and policy-making, there is a surprising lack of concrete, data-driven evidence to support it in a structured and comparative way.

This project, carried out as part of the Open Access and Digital Ethics course at the University of Bologna, aims to investigate the relationship between the number and types of vehicles on the road and air quality levels in Milan and Bologna. By combining traffic data (including vehicle counts and categories) with historical air pollution measurements, we intend to analyze whether a statistically significant correlation exists, and if so, how it varies across different urban contexts and pollutants. The goal is to move beyond assumptions and provide empirical insights that can inform more effective environmental and mobility policies.

DATASETS

To carry out our analysis, we relied on a set of core datasets from which we extracted various subsets tailored to our specific needs. These main datasets — Open Data - Comune di Bologna, I numeri di Bologna metropolitana, and Open Data - Comune di Milano — provided the foundational data necessary to explore and address the objectives of our study.

ANALYSIS

As part of our work, we conducted several complementary analyses to evaluate the datasets from different perspectives. These included a quality analysis to assess the reliability and completeness of the data, a legal analysis to examine licensing and usage rights, an ethics analysis to consider potential implications and biases, a technical analysis to evaluate data formats and accessibility, and an assessment of the sustainability of dataset updates to understand how regularly and reliably the data is maintained.
